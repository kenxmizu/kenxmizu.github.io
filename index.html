Studying literature is really how one can say the best words to fit the ideas in their thoughts. Articulation, not in the sounds of words but the words themselves. Thoughts are elusive in nature, and we can never access pure thoughts directly, or rather we can, but others can never, nor can we ever accurately communicate these thoughts, only a diluted or slanted version of it. I have a feeling that these thoughts are very much dense and vibrant and transient. Try to take a grasp of it and you will only find yourself with a fistful of nothing; well, not really but with a very minute detail or apprehension of something. Throughout the course of history in literature, countless writers have tried to crack this enigma of thought and language and articulation (although not limited to writers as there are numerous other mediums of expression and articulation of thought) and they have come a long way.
